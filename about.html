Our Story

Founders' Story

Leilani & Koa Maka — A Dream Washed Clean

Growing up in Waipahu, Leilani Maka always noticed families lugging overstuffed garbage bags to a dingy, coin-only laundromat two towns over. She knew her community deserved better.

In 2018, Leilani and her husband Koa pooled their savings, secured a Small Business Administration loan, and converted a vacant Farrington Hwy retail space into what is now Maile Fresh Laundry — bright, welcoming, modern, and deeply local.

They named it after the maile vine, a Hawaiian plant used in lei for honoring loved ones, because they wanted every customer to feel honored and cared for from the moment they walked in.

Today, Maile Fresh serves hundreds of Waipahu families each week, employs eight local residents, and has expanded to offer dry cleaning and professional alteration services — all under one roof.
