The Complete Guide to Washing Your Surf Gear

Surf Lifestyle June 12, 2024 · By Leilani Maka

Surfing is part of life in Hawaii, and with that lifestyle comes a unique laundry challenge: saltwater, sunscreen, sand, and constant moisture are brutal on wetsuits, rash guards, board shorts, and beach towels. At Maile Fresh Laundry in Waipahu, we see surf gear every single day — here's how to clean it properly so it lasts season after season.

Why Surf Gear Needs Special Care

The combination of salt, UV exposure, chlorine (if you also swim in pools), and bacteria from the ocean creates a hostile environment for fabric. Left untreated, salt crystals break down neoprene fibers, sunscreen degrades elastic, and odors become permanently embedded. The good news: a proper rinse and wash routine extends the life of your gear dramatically.

Wetsuits: The Most Delicate Item

Your wetsuit is an investment — many cost $200–$600 or more. Proper care protects that investment.

  • Rinse immediately after every session with fresh water, front and back, inside and out.
  • Never use hot water on neoprene — it degrades the rubber and shortens flexibility.
  • Use wetsuit-specific shampoo or a very mild soap. Never use regular detergent.
  • Do not wring or fold tightly — store rolled loosely or hung on a wide hanger.
  • Dry inside out in the shade. UV rays degrade neoprene over time.
  • Never machine wash a wetsuit — the agitation can tear seams and damage rubber.

Rash Guards and Swim Shirts

Rash guards are typically made from spandex/Lycra blends — they're durable but require cold-water washing to maintain stretch and color.

  • Cold water, gentle cycle only
  • Avoid fabric softener — it coats the UPF fibers and reduces sun protection
  • Air dry flat or hang — tumble drying breaks down elastic fibers
  • Turn inside out before washing to protect printed designs

Board Shorts

Most board shorts are quick-dry polyester or nylon. They're more durable than rash guards but still benefit from cold-water washing. Check the label — some shorts have mesh liners that can snag in agitators. Use a mesh laundry bag if machine washing.

Beach Towels

Rinse beach towels thoroughly after each use to remove sand and salt. Wash with warm water (not hot) and a full measure of detergent. Shake out sand before putting in the washer — sand particles abrade fabric fibers over time. Our XL washers at Maile Fresh are perfect for large beach towels and won't damage them the way small residential machines can.

"We always tell surfers: rinse in the parking lot, then properly wash at least every other session. The salt really does a number on gear if you let it dry in." — Danny Akana, Maile Fresh Tech

Sunscreen Stains

Mineral sunscreens (zinc oxide) are the worst for stains — they leave white or yellowish residue that's hard to remove once set. Pre-treat with a small amount of dish soap directly on the stain before washing, and use warm (not hot) water. Avoid hot drying until the stain is fully gone.
